Nepal Unveils Historic Rs 2.124 Trillion Budget — Biggest in Nation's History

June 16, 2026 · Economy · Sandesh Desk

Finance Minister Dr. Swarnim Wagle presented Nepal's largest-ever federal budget of Rs 2,124.34 billion for Fiscal Year 2026/27 in a joint sitting of Parliament — a 25.2% expansion over the current year's revised estimates. The budget targets 7% economic growth, caps inflation at 6%, and promises sweeping tax reforms, massive infrastructure investment and an overhaul of public institutions. Civil servant salaries will rise approximately 21%, with minimum monthly pay ranging from Rs 40,000 to Rs 100,000. Business groups have welcomed the package but warn weak inter-agency coordination could jeopardise the ambitious targets.
